EQUIPMENT

  • Tent or Hammock

    This will serve as your primary shelter. There will be pros and cons and it all comes down to preferences and the location of the camping site.

  • Sleeping bag

    A sleeping bag is the fine line to a good night at the camp from a bad one. It also provides insulation from the cold to prevent sickness and hypothermia.

  • Camping stove, cookware, and utensils.

    Sustenance is second to none in any camping activity and a warm bowl of food is the best way to end the trek, also a fresh brewed cup of coffee never hurt no one. These should also inlude your fire starters and knives.

  • Sleeping pad and travel pillow.

    These could be called luxuries in a campsite you can bring these for extra comfort during the night or for quick naps in the mountains.

    • CAMPING SAFETY

    • Be of any risk around your tent or campsite
    • Bring enough sustenance for the duration of your camp.
    • Keep a lookout for the weather.

    • Prepare a first aid kit.
    • Be aware of dangerous wildlife.
    • Keep safety around fires in the campsite

      CAMPING SPOTS

      • Camp Amadura

        A wide grassland in the Cadiz City. It gives you an overlooking view of the northern part of Negros Occidental with only a 15 minute hike from the drop point.

      • Camp Forrest Springs

        A relaxing place at the mountain barangay of Patag. It offers a wide place filled with local hardwood. Perfect for car and motorcampers as it is very accesible for vehicles.

      • Minuyan, Murcia

        A local hotspot for campsites in Negros Occidental. All the campsite offers breathtaking hikes, a view of the city lights on the northern part of Negros and lots of falls and springs to relax from.
